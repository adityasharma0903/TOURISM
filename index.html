LEH LADAKH

Leh Ladakh Overview Location: City in Indian-administered Ladakh, disputed Kashmir region.
Status: Largest city and joint capital of Ladakh.
Historical Significance: Former capital of the Kingdom of Ladakh; Leh Palace built in the same style as Potala Palace in Tibet.
Altitude: 3,524 meters (11,562 feet).
Geography
River: Located on the bank of the Indus River.
Mountains: Dominated by mountains, with peaks like Nanga Sago reaching above 5,500 meters.
Access Roads: Srinagar-Leh Highway (434 km) and Leh-Manali Highway (428 km), both seasonal.
Climate
Type: Cold desert climate.
Winters: Long, cold winters from late November to early March, with temperatures well below freezing.
Snowfall: Occasional snowfall during winter.
Other Seasons: Fine and warm weather during the day in remaining months.
Rainfall: Average annual rainfall is 35 mm (1.37 inches).
Transportation
Roads:
National Highway 1: Srinagar to Leh, open from April/May to October/November.
Leh-Manali Highway: Open from June to late October, traverses high passes and plateaus.
Nimmu–Padam–Darcha Road: Under construction as of 2024.
Air: Leh Kushok Bakula Rimpochee Airport with flights to Delhi, Jammu, Srinagar, and Chandigarh.
Rail: No current railway service; proposed routes include Bhanupli–Leh line and Srinagar–Kargil–Leh line.
